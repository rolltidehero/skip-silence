## Long description (Chrome Web Store / AMO)

**Ever notice how much of a lecture is… nothing?**

Pauses, dead air, the professor writing on the board, the podcast host taking
a breath. Skip Silence listens to what you're playing and fast-forwards
through the quiet parts — then drops back to normal speed the instant someone
speaks. You hear every word, just without the waiting.

**How it works**

1. Play any video or podcast.
2. Skip Silence analyzes the audio in real time, right in your browser.
3. During silence, playback speeds up (you pick how much — 2×, 3×, more).
4. When speech returns, it snaps back to your normal speed instantly.

How much you save depends on the content: a lecture full of pauses shrinks
noticeably, a tightly edited video less so. The extension keeps a running
count of the time it has saved you.

**Features**

- Works on YouTube, Twitch VODs, Coursera, podcast players and any site with
  HTML5 video or audio
- Separate speeds for speech and silence — fully adjustable
- Optional muting of silent parts, so sped-up background noise stays inaudible
- Smooth transitions: a small audio lookahead unmutes exactly when speech
  returns, so soft first words aren't clipped
- Automatic threshold that adapts to each video's noise floor — no tuning
  needed (manual mode with a live volume meter if you want control)
- Per-site settings: different setup for your lecture portal than for
  YouTube, or off entirely on music sites
- Keyboard shortcut: Ctrl+Shift+S to toggle anywhere
- Live volume meter in the popup shows exactly what's being detected

**Private by design**

Everything happens locally in your browser. No account, no analytics, no
audio or browsing data ever leaves your machine. The extension needs only
storage (for your settings) — it has no access to your data beyond the media
it analyzes on the page.

**Good to know**

DRM-protected services (Netflix, Spotify) don't allow extensions to analyze
their audio, and live streams are intentionally left alone. Skip Silence
tells you in the popup whenever a page can't be analyzed.

## Claims checklist (keep us honest)

- ✅ "Runs entirely locally / no data collection" — true, there is no
  analytics or network code.
- ✅ "Works on any site with HTML5 media" — with the documented exceptions
  (DRM, live streams, some cross-origin embeds); the copy discloses this.
- ⚠️ Never promise a specific time saving ("save 25%") — it depends on
  content. Illustrate instead.
- ⚠️ Don't claim Netflix/Spotify support anywhere.
